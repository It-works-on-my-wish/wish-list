We evaluated our extraction system on 20 different product pages to measure its performance across key fields: name, price, and image. The results show that name extraction achieved the highest accuracy (16/20), indicating that the model is relatively reliable in identifying product titles. Price extraction performed moderately well (14/20); however, since price is a critical piece of information for end users, improving this component should be a top priority. Image extraction had the lowest accuracy (11/20), suggesting that the system struggles more with identifying and selecting the correct visual content from HTML data.

At the field level, the system correctly extracted 41 out of 60 total fields, corresponding to an overall field-level accuracy of approximately 68.3%. When considering complete correctness—where all three fields (name, price, and image) are accurately extracted for a given product—the system achieved a complete extraction rate of 9 out of 20. Additionally, the failure rate was relatively low (4/20), indicating that the system is generally robust and able to return at least partial results in most cases.

Overall, these results suggest that while the system performs strongly in name extraction and maintains a reasonable level of robustness, improvements are particularly needed in image extraction and, more importantly, in price extraction due to its high significance in practical use cases.
